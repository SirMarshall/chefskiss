Instructions

01

Prepare the Marinade

In a small bowl, whisk together the white miso paste, mirin, soy sauce, and a teaspoon of sesame oil until smooth. If the mixture is too thick, add a splash of water to loosen it up.

02

Marinate the Salmon

Place the salmon fillets in a shallow dish or ziplock bag. Pour the marinade over the fish, ensuring each piece is well coated. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator, or up to 2 hours for deeper flavor.

03

Cook Rice & Prep Veggies

While the salmon marinates, cook your jasmine rice according to package instructions. Slice the avocados, chop the green onions, and steam the edamame if using frozen.

04

Roast the Salmon

Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the marinated salmon on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Roast for 12-15 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork. For a caramelized top, broil for the last 1-2 minutes.

lightbulb

Chef's Tip: Keep an eye on it while broiling, the miso glaze contains sugar and can burn quickly!

05

Assemble & Serve

Divide the rice among bowls. Top with the glazed salmon, edamame, and avocado slices. Garnish generously with sesame seeds and green onions. Drizzle with any remaining pan juices.
